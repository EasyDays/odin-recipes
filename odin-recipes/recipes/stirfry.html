Stir Fry

Bowl of Very Good Stir Fry

Easy Vegetable Stir Fry is a mixture of colorful vegetables sautéed in a sweet and savory sauce that makes for a simple weeknight meal! Less than 30 minutes to make from start to finish!

Ingredients

  • 1 lb protein (chicken, beef, tofu, shrimp, etc.), sliced
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas, etc.), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon hoisin sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Steps:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, sesame oil, cornstarch, and chicken/vegetable broth. Set aside.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over high heat.
  3. Add the sliced protein and cook until browned and cooked through. Remove from the wok and set aside.
  4. In the same wok, add a bit more oil if needed. Add the minced garlic and ginger. Sauté for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the chopped mixed vegetables to the wok. Stir-fry for a few minutes until they start to become tender but still crisp.
  6. Return the cooked protein to the wok with the vegetables.
  7. Pour the sauce mixture over the stir-fry. Toss everything together to coat with the sauce and let it thicken.
  8. Once the sauce is thickened and the ingredients are well-coated, remove from heat.
  9. Serve the stir-fry over cooked rice or noodles.
  10. Enjoy your delicious homemade stir-fry!
